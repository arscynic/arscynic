People

Good wizards worth listening to more often than not.

  • Wondering Freely. “I am a philosopher. I hold the Danforth Chair in the Humanities and these are musings on philosophy, religion, science, art, literature and other topics.” —Helen De Cruz
  • The Renaissance Mathematicus. “An aging freak who fell in love with the history of science and now lives mostly in the 16th century.”
  • Klaas Mensaert. Bioinformatician applying scientific insights to improve how we go about democracy.
  • Pierre de Buyl. Physicist gone FOSS.
  • Nicky Case. Prodigious educator.

Video

  • Horses. “Essays by Michael Sorensen.”
  • Ordinary Things. “Everything is interesting.”
  • Second Thought. “Education and analysis of current events from a socialist perspective.”
  • Last Week Tonight. John Oliver delivering “breaking news on a weekly basis.”
  • Good Work. Hilarious “edge-cutting business news.”
  • Kurzgesagt. Research brought through beautiful animations.

Podcasts

None of the podcasts below contain ads.

  • The History of English Podcast. “Kevin Stroud presents the history of English by exploring the historical events that shaped the modern language, as well as the history of English words and grammar.”
  • Well There's Your Problem. “This is a podcast about engineering disasters and systemic failures, from a leftist perspective, with jokes.”
  • You're Wrong About. “Mike and Sarah are journalists obsessed with the past. Every week they reconsider an event, person or phenomenon that’s been miscast in the public imagination.”
  • The Taxcast. “The Tax Justice Network podcast on corruption, tax havens and financial secrecy: transformational economic analysis you won’t find anywhere else on how to fix our world.”
  • Titans of Nuclear. A podcast on nuclear energy.
  • The Joy of Why. “The mathematician and author Steven Strogatz and the astrophysicist and author Janna Levin interview leading researchers about the great scientific and mathematical questions of our time.”
